## Philosophy card
"When you're on a busy schedule, highly structured practice can feel cumbersome and boring.
But loose, irregular practice makes it hard to know what to do — and leaves you feeling
unsatisfied with your progress.

The Log Book follows a specific pre-defined method built around **consistency**. You can modify
the routine to your own goals, but the structure stays the same: a spine you always do, focus
areas you rotate through, and weekly goals that give each session a direction. There's enough
structure to make practices worthwhile — and enough flexibility to adapt to real life."

## Feature: The Spine
"Start every session with a short warmup of 3–5 essential exercises — technique, sight-reading,
memorization. If you only have 15 minutes, just do this. The spine ensures you always get
something done, no matter how busy the day is."

Tip: "Add things that benefit from repetition. Uncheck an exercise when you need a break — you can always bring it back."

## Feature: Focus Areas
"Set up to 3 big-picture focus areas (e.g. Guitar, Voice, Songwriting) and choose a daily focus.
Each area has its own pool of active exercises. On slower days, pick 'Free' and practice without
an agenda — no guilt."

Tip: "Keep 3–5 exercises active per area. Park interesting exercises you find online in reserve, unchecked, for when you want to shake things up."

## Feature: Weekly Logs
"Some practice goals span multiple sessions — working through all 12 keys, learning a position
on the neck, finishing a piece. Set your focus at the start of the week and check in each session."

Tip: "Set up to 3 weekly focus areas in Settings. Update them each Monday in the Start Week menu."
