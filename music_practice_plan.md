# Music Practice Plan

A unified practice routine covering guitar fundamentals, ear training, voice, songwriting, and weekly posting. Built around a daily spine (the minimum) plus a choose-your-own-adventure day-type system.

---

## Daily Practice

### Step 1 — What kind of day?

- **Knowledge** — theory and fretboard work
- **Creative** — applied / output work
- **Voice** — voice as primary instrument
- **Free** — spine only, then whatever

### Step 2 — Pick 1-2 focus exercises

The spine still applies on Knowledge, Creative, and Voice days. On Voice days, swap the guitar technique warmup for a vocal warmup (lip trills, sirens).

#### Knowledge Day

**Focus:**
1. **Scale, saying notes** — week's scale in focus position with metronome, name notes aloud
2. **Triad inversions** — I, IV, V on one string set, all three inversions, walk a progression

**Reserve:**
- Scale, singing degrees
- Modes via scale (start from each degree)
- Triads in position (all diatonic triads within the scale shape)
- Single-string scale (Goodrick)

#### Creative Day

**Focus:**
1. **Scale-only improv** — backing track in the week's key, scale position only
2. **Songwriting fragment** — write 4 bars or just capture an idea in a voice memo

**Reserve:**
- Chord-tone improv
- Sing-play trading
- Transcription deep dive
- Improvise then transcribe

#### Voice Day
Always warm up first (3-5 min of lip trills and sirens).

**Focus:**
1. **Song work** — pick a song, work small sections, record and listen back
2. **Sing the transcription** — week's transcription phrase with dynamics and phrasing

**Reserve:**
- Range work
- Breath support
- Drone matching
- Sing scale degrees

#### Free Day
Spine only. Then play whatever you want. No agenda, no key-of-the-week.

---

## Daily Spine

Non-negotiable on Knowledge, Creative, and Voice days. Times below are floors, not budgets — go longer wherever the practice grabs you.

- **Technique warmup** — at least 2-3 min, rote speed exercise with metronome (see Speed Practice below)
- **Fretboard Trainer** — at least 2 min, rotating string focus (currently weakest: D, G, B)
- **Leavitt reading** — one new exercise + previous review
- **Transcription** — at least one phrase. Sing it, notate it, then play it.

On Voice Day, swap the technique warmup for a vocal warmup. The rest of the spine still applies.

If a day gets crushed, doing only the spine still counts as a successful practice.

---

## Weekly Schedule

- 5 practice days — pick the day type when you sit down; any of them can run long if it's flowing
- Saturday — posting ritual
- Sunday — off

Aim for Knowledge and Creative each ~1-2x per week, Voice ~1-2x per week, Free as needed. No fixed day-to-type mapping. No forced long session — let one emerge naturally when you're in flow.

---

## Speed / Technique Practice

Daily small reps beat occasional long grinds. Cap at ~5 min on short days.

**Focus:**
1. **1-2-3-4 chromatic** — across all strings, strict alternate picking
2. **Spider exercise** — 1-3-2-4 and other permutations

**Reserve:**
- Three-notes-per-string scale runs at increasing tempos
- Single-note tremolo / alternate picking for endurance
- Trill drills (hammer-on/pull-off between two notes, 30-sec bursts)

**The protocol:**

1. Set the metronome to a tempo where you can play it **cleanly** — not "mostly clean," actually clean.
2. Play it for 1-2 minutes at that tempo.
3. Bump the metronome up by 3-5 BPM.
4. If still clean, that's your new working tempo. If sloppy, drop back down.

**Core principle: clean before fast.** Pushing past clean tempo just trains sloppiness faster. The "rote" is daily reps; the "speed" is slow, incremental tempo creep.

**Track:** current exercise, current clean tempo, last bump date.

**Notes:**

- Plateaus are real. Stuck for a week? Drop 10 BPM, play there for a few days, ramp back up. The deload often produces the breakthrough.
- Any tightness or pain → stop immediately. Speed work is the #1 cause of guitarist overuse injury.

## Key & Concept Rotation

One key, one concept per week. The key is the anchor that connects scales, triads, improv, singing, and songwriting.

**First cycle (example):**

- Week 1 — G major: scale + major triads on top 3 strings
- Week 2 — E minor: natural minor + minor triads on top 3 strings
- Week 3 — D major: scale + triads on middle 3 strings
- Week 4 — A minor: integrate, start adding 7ths to known triads

After the first cycle, work through more keys, deepening the concept layer (other string sets, modes via context, drop 2 voicings, 7th arpeggios, etc.).

---

## Singing-While-Playing Map

Even outside Voice Days, singing is built into the guitar practice:

- **Transcription** — sing the phrase before notating, sing again after playing
- **Scales** — sing scale degrees or note names along with playing
- **Triads** — sing root, 3rd, 5th as you play each inversion
- **Improv** — sing along, or trade sung lines with played lines
- **Songwriting** — already built in

A 2-3 min vocal warmup before any session with lots of singing prevents bad habits.

---

## Posting Notes

- One post per week. Saturday is the default ritual day.
- Format consistency matters more than novelty: stick with one format (e.g., acoustic guitar + vocal, 30-60 sec, one take) for at least a month before changing.
- Phone audio is fine. Don't let production quality become a gating factor.
- Source material: transcriptions, songwriting sketches, covers, riffs from the week's practice.
- The first 20-50 posts are for you, not the audience. Measure by: did I post, did I learn something, is the next one easier?

---

## Compression Rules

When a day gets short, drop in this order:

1. Keep the spine — always
2. Keep one focus exercise from the chosen day type, abbreviated
3. Skip the rest, no guilt

**Other rules:**

- Missed a day? Don't make it up. Resume.
- Post skipped? Skip it. Post next week.
- Streak isn't the goal. Cumulative reps are.

---

## Priority Order (if a week gets compressed)

1. Daily spine (technique, fretboard, reading, transcription)
2. Posting ritual (deadline pressure produces work)
3. Focus exercises in chosen day types

---

## Core Principles

- **Always say note names or sing chord tones aloud** while practicing scales and triads. This wires fretboard knowledge to theory.
- **Practice in keys, not in shapes.** Forces real thinking instead of autopilot.
- **Use a metronome from day one.** Not optional.
- **Record everything.** Voice memos of practice ideas become content fuel and idea archive.
- **Don't grind one phase to perfection before moving on.** Once something is ~80% solid, layer in the next thing.
- **Cross-pollinate the week's key.** Scales, triads, improv, singing, songwriting all in the same key reinforces everything.

---

## Review After 4 Weeks

After one full key cycle (~4 weeks), check:

- What's actually getting done vs. drifting?
- Are sessions sustainable?
- Is posting happening weekly?
- Are the focus exercises still serving you, or should you swap from reserve?
- What needs to be adjusted?

Then tweak and run the next cycle.
